Sequential disbursement protocol

Code98 operates a deterministic, chronologically‚Äëstrict queue abstraction for each discrete denomination. Upon receipt of consideration, the transactor's payload is appended to the tail of the denomination‚Äëspecific FIFO queue. The system's state machine continuously evaluates the head of each queue; upon accumulation of the requisite counterparty consideration, an irrevocable disbursement instruction is executed via the underlying payment rails, transferring the contractual return amount to the original remitter and the administrative fee to the designated operational account.

‚è≥ First-In, First-Out (FIFO) axiomatic invariance

2.1 Upon successful validation of an incoming consideration, the system shall atomically append the Participant's details to the tail of the queue corresponding to the exact amount remitted. 3.2 The disbursement instruction comprises two simultaneous components: (a) the return consideration to the Participant (as contractually determined) and (b) the administrative fee to the designated operational account. 3.3 Participants acknowledge that while the system commits to disbursement within milliseconds of the trigger condition, the actual reflection of value in the recipient's mobile money wallet depends on the processing characteristics of the mobile network operator and any inter‚Äëscheme clearing mechanisms. 4. DENOMINATION INTEGRITY AND QUEUE DISCRETENESS

4.1 The system enforces strict denomination integrity: a consideration of 50 KES can only be paired with another 50 KES consideration for the purpose of triggering a disbursement. No mixing, averaging, or aggregation across denominations shall occur. This ensures that the probabilistic payout model remains mathematically consistent and administratively predictable.
4.2 Participants selecting a specific denomination manifest their assent to the queue dynamics and expected latency inherent to that denomination's volume.
